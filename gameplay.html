The Interface

This game's interface is very unique, split into four sections, each governing a different element of the game. Each element is relevant in moment-to-moment gameplay, so having them each visible at all times is ideal for readability and gameplay.

Top Left shows the player's view of Fid, this is where you navigate inside of rooms, interact, and fight.

Top Right is the 3D map of the surrounding area, showing rooms, features, allies and enemies, and other important information. This interface is not just for reading, you can select rooms and move them, for navigation or to create an advantage in combat.

Bottom Left is the circuitboard wiring interface. Here Fid can rewire their circuits to install new upgrades or improve power efficiency. This interface also shows other in-world circuits that you can interact with.

Bottom Right is the global map, it displays a map of all the cities in the world, and important information about troop movements, and mission locations.

The World

Orotof is made up of 11 cities, which you can travel between via green city exits. Cities are connected by highways, trains, tunnels, or roads.

The struggle for power in the game is based around cotrol of each city, giving you the advantage of what the city has, and which cities this city is connected to. This can increate a faction's mobility, and also cut off other factions from certain parts of the country, slowing troop movement and resupplyment.

Orotof sits in a valley lined by two mountain ranges, which both provide protection from Camto, and isolate Orotof from their eastern neighbors.

The capital city Katoa, sits in a comfortable place in the north of the valley, close to their coprporate allies in Rakatland. While southern cities are more focused on production and industry, Katoa is a metropolis, facilitating trade, governance, and mining from a safe distance.


The insides of cities are made up of Rooms, which can have a variety of shapes and sizes, functions, and contents.

The top left screen shows Fid and the inside of the current room. The rooms will contain enemies and interactables.

Rooms are connected via doors, holes, and elevators.

Some rooms have special properties that make them behave differently than typical rooms. If a room is special, it cannot be moved via Room Displacement.

Special rooms come in many forms, being public infrastructure (electric substation, water reclamation center) that factions must maintain control over; or methods of traversal like train lines or elevators.

These rooms are often arranged to create small puzzles for the player, and through destruction mechanics, emergent puzzles may be created. These are unintented, yet are still solveable.

Room Displacement

One important thing about Fid's 3D map of the nearby rooms: it is not read-only.

In the 3D viewport you can click on rooms to select them, then move them to a more desirable position. Because you are navigating an active warzone, some rooms may become unusable or destroyed, cutting you off from your destination.

To get around this, you can move most rooms (that you are not inside of) to create a new path for you, and your allies.

You can also harness this power to cut off your enemies from attacking sensitive areas, or wherever their destination may be.

Circuitry

The main character Fid was an electrician before the war, and those skills are not going to waste.

In the circuit view, you can pick up and move circuit boards, and connect wires to them. All of this is snapped to a grid of points.

Circuitry in HOMELAND doesn't work quite the same as real life:

    Each circuit consumes a certain amount of power

    Remainin power will exit it's output and can be distributed further

    When power exits an output, it is evenly distributed between all connected inputs

    Every input has an amount of draw, which could starve earlier circuits

Circuitry is still in development

Combat

Combat takes place inside of the rooms between you and enemy NPCs. You can attack anybody, so consider who it is that you would most like to attack.

Fighting can be done through melee or ranged weapons, or using abilities given through your circuits.

Any of the other mechanics can be used to give yourself an advantage in combat, so get creative with different tactics!

Missions

The gameplay is driven by missions, given to you by factions you have good relations with.

Choosing to accept or deny missions is your agency in the outcome of the war, whomever you assist will be given a massive advantage.

There are several types of missions:

    Taking control of an area

    Repairing Infrastructure

    Delivering supplies

    Escorting civilians

    Disrupting supply lines

You might run into any number of unexpected confrontations in getting to the mission, or during the missions; be wary.
